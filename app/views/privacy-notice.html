Who collects your data

The controllers collecting your personal data are the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) and the Environmental Agency. Defra and the Environmental Agency are committed to the responsible handling and security of personal data. Your privacy is important to us and protected in law through the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), The Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018) and the Law Enforcement Directive.

If you have any questions about how Defra and the Environment Agency intend to use your personal data as set out in this Privacy Notice, and your associated rights under data protection law, email: dataprotection@environment-agency.gov.uk.

The data we collect

The personal information we collect includes:

  • your name
  • contact details, which may include a home address, email address and telephone numbers
  • the details of your complaint

We do not process special category data.

How your data is used

Your data is used by official authorities (Defra and the Environmental Agency) to provide us with information on how Walleys Quarry is affecting you. We use your personal data to respond to you regarding your incident report if necessary.

We do not:

  • use your data to make an automated decision or for automated profiling
  • share your personal data with anyone outside the Environment Agency without your consent, unless we must do so by law
  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with thirds parties for marketing purposes

We will only share information when necessary to meet the statutory requirements of the Environmental Information Regulations 2004 and The Freedom of Information Act 2000. Our Personal Information Charter explains more about how your data is processed.

How long your data is held

We will keep your personal data while the incident is ongoing and for up to seven years after its conclusion.

Where your data is processed and stored

We store your data in Dataverse (a web application that helps us analyse research data) hosted either in the UK (London or Cardiff) or in Europe (Amsterdam or Dublin). Data is not transferred outside the European Economic Area. Data is stored in our National Incident Reporting System (NIRS) in Milton Keynes.

Contact details

Our Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for independent advice and monitoring of the Environment Agency's use of data and information.

If you have any concerns or questions about how we process personal data, or if you would like to make a complaint or request relating to data protection, email our DPO: dataprotection@environment-agency.gov.uk.

You can write to The Data Protection Officer, Environment Agency, Horizon House, Deanery Road, Bristol, BS1 5AH.

The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) regulates the data protection legislation. The ICO asks that you try to resolve any data protection concerns with the DPO in the first instance before complaining. You have the right to lodge a complaint with the ICO at any time.

This Privacy Notice was last updated on 1 September 2021.
